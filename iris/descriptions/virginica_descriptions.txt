<p><i><b>Iris virginica</b></i>, with the common name <b>Virginia iris</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Perennial" class="mw-redirect" title="Perennial">perennial</a> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plant</a>, native to eastern North America.</p>
<p>It is common along the <a href="/wiki/Coastal_plain" title="Coastal plain">coastal plain</a> from <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> to <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_United_States" title="Southeastern United States">Southeastern United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-usda_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usda-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It is one of the three <i>Iris</i> species in the <a href="/wiki/Iris_flower_data_set" title="Iris flower data set"><i>Iris</i> flower data set</a> outlined by <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Fisher" title="Ronald Fisher">Ronald Fisher</a> in his 1936 paper "The use of multiple measurements in taxonomic problems" as an example of <a href="/wiki/Linear_discriminant_analysis" title="Linear discriminant analysis">linear discriminant analysis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fisher36_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fisher36-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
